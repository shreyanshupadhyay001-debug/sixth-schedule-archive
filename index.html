"The Governor may, by public notification — include any area in Part I of the said Table, create a new autonomous district, increase the area of any autonomous district, diminish the area of any autonomous district, unite two or more autonomous districts or parts thereof so as to form one autonomous district, define the boundaries of any autonomous district."
Dr. B.R. Ambedkar, moving Amendment No. 99 to Paragraph 1 of the Sixth Schedule — 5th September 1949
Schedule Timeline
1949
Original Sixth Schedule adopted in Constituent Assembly on 5–7 September 1949
1950
Constitution of India comes into force; Sixth Schedule operative in Assam
1972
Meghalaya, Manipur and Tripura gain statehood; Schedule extended
1987
Mizoram and Arunachal Pradesh become states; Sixth Schedule applies to Mizoram
2003
Bodoland Territorial Council established under Sixth Schedule in Assam
2023
Constitutional Amendment Bill proposed to extend Sixth Schedule to new territories
